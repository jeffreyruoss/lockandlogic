# What Makes an Escape Room Website Work

> Client-facing document

This is a breakdown of every feature a modern escape room website can have, organized by how important it is to your business. We use this to make sure we're building the right things at launch and planning smart for the future.

---

## Must-Have at Launch

These are non-negotiable. Without them, the website won't do its job.

| Feature | What it does for you |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Online booking** | Customers pick a room, choose a date and time, and pay — all from their phone or computer. No back-and-forth calls or emails. |
| **Room pages** | Each room gets its own page with photos, a description, difficulty level, group size, duration, and price. This is how customers decide which room to book. |
| **Clear pricing** | Prices are visible upfront. Hidden pricing is the #1 reason people leave an escape room website without booking. |
| **Mobile-friendly design** | Over 60% of your visitors will be on their phones. If the site doesn't work well on mobile, you lose the majority of potential bookings. |
| **Location & contact info** | Your address, phone, email, hours, and an embedded Google Map — on every page. Customers need to know where you are and how to reach you. |
| **"How it works" section** | A clear explanation for first-timers: what to expect, how the game works, what to bring, when to arrive. Reduces anxiety and gets more people to book. |
| **SEO foundation** | The technical setup that helps you show up when someone searches "escape room near me" or "escape room Pottstown." Without this, you're invisible online. |
| **Analytics** | Tracking that shows how many people visit your site, where they come from, and where they drop off. You can't improve what you don't measure. |
| **Atmospheric design** | Your website is the first impression of your experience. If it looks generic, people assume the rooms will be too. A dark, immersive design signals quality. |
| **Legal pages** | Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. Required by law for any site collecting personal data and processing payments. |

## Strongly Recommended

These have a direct impact on bookings and revenue. We include most of these in your build.

| Feature | What it does for you |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Gift cards** | Customers buy digital gift cards online. Escape rooms are one of the most popular experiential gifts — this is easy revenue, especially around holidays and birthdays. |
| **Private vs. public booking** | Lets customers choose whether they'll share their session with strangers. "Will we be with other people?" is the most common question escape rooms get. |
| **Corporate / team building page** | A dedicated page that speaks directly to companies looking for team events. Corporate groups are the highest-value customers — larger groups, premium pricing, and they book on weekdays. |
| **Reviews & testimonials** | Displaying your Google rating and customer quotes on the site. People rely heavily on reviews before booking an experience they can't preview. |
| **Google Business Profile** | Your listing on Google Maps and Search. When someone searches "escape room near me," this is what they see first — before your website. |
| **Digital waivers** | Customers sign a liability waiver online before they arrive, instead of spending 10 minutes doing paperwork at check-in. Better experience, less hassle for staff. |
| **Automated email reminders** | Confirmation emails when they book, reminders before their visit, and a follow-up after. Reduces no-shows and drives reviews. |
| **FAQ page** | Answers the questions people have before booking: Can we bring kids? What if we're late? What's the cancellation policy? Removes hesitation. |
| **Newsletter signup** | An email list you own. You can notify subscribers about new rooms, promotions, and events. It's the most cost-effective marketing channel. |
| **Accessibility info** | Clear information about wheelchair access, mobility requirements, and any physical considerations for each room. Builds trust and avoids negative surprises. |
| **Structured data** | Behind-the-scenes code that tells Google to show your star rating, price range, and hours directly in search results. Most competitors don't do this — it's a real advantage. |
| **Custom 404 page** | When someone hits a broken link, they see a branded page with helpful links instead of a dead end. Small detail, professional touch. |

## Nice-to-Have

These separate good escape room websites from great ones. Not essential at launch, but worth considering as you grow.

| Feature | What it does for you |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Birthday party page** | A dedicated page for birthday bookings with party packages and add-ons. Parents search specifically for "escape room birthday party." Can be handled through the group inquiry form at first. |
| **Photo gallery** | A standalone page showcasing your rooms, venue, and happy teams. Goes beyond the photos on individual room pages. |
| **Post-game photo sharing** | Branded team photos taken after each game, sent to customers with social sharing buttons. Every share is free advertising with your name on it. |
| **Leaderboard** | Top escape times displayed on the site, filterable by room. Drives repeat bookings ("we need to beat our time") and social sharing. |
| **Blog** | Articles about escape room tips, team building, behind-the-scenes content. Each post is a new way for people to find you through Google. |
| **Live chat** | A chat widget for quick pre-booking questions. Answers "Can I bring my 10-year-old?" in real time instead of losing the customer. |
| **Checkout upsells** | Add-on options during booking: photo packages, merchandise, extra clues. Increases what each customer spends. |
| **Loyalty program** | Reward repeat visits — especially natural for escape rooms since customers want to try every room. |
| **Referral program** | Give customers a reason to tell friends: "Refer 5 friends, get a free game." Turns happy customers into a marketing channel. |
| **Abandoned booking recovery** | Automated email when someone starts booking but doesn't finish. Handled by the booking platform, not the website. |
| **Social media integration** | Instagram feed, customer photos, and branded hashtags displayed on the site. Authentic, constantly refreshing content. |
| **Video trailers** | Short cinematic videos for each room. Video conveys atmosphere better than photos and gets shared more on social media. |

## Future / Growth Features

For when the business scales. Not relevant at launch, but good to know they exist.

| Feature | What it does for you |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Dynamic pricing** | Automatically charge more on Friday nights and less on Tuesday afternoons. Maximizes revenue from every time slot. |
| **Multi-location management** | Run multiple venues from one dashboard. Essential if you open a second location. |
| **Advanced analytics** | Deep reporting on revenue by room, occupancy rates, marketing attribution, and seasonal trends. |
| **Customer database (CRM)** | Track customer history, segment your audience, and personalize marketing. |
| **Staff scheduling** | Tie game master schedules to the booking calendar so you always have the right staffing. |
| **Third-party integrations** | Connect your booking system to accounting software, travel platforms (TripAdvisor, Viator), and marketing tools. |
| **A/B testing** | Test different versions of your booking page to find what converts best. |
| **Automated review requests** | Post-visit emails asking customers to leave a Google review. Fresh reviews help you rank higher in local search. |
| **Merchandise shop** | Sell branded merch (t-shirts, puzzles, mugs) online. |
| **Multi-language support** | Website in multiple languages. Only relevant if you're in a tourist-heavy area. |
