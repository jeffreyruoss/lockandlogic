## 1. Essential (Must-Have for Launch)

These features are non-negotiable. Without them, the site will not function as a viable booking platform for an escape room business.

### 1.1 Online Booking System

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Real-time availability calendar with date/time slot selection, room picker, group size selector, and integrated payment processing. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms live and die by online bookings — if customers cannot see availability and pay quickly, they leave. A streamlined, mobile-friendly checkout (minimal clicks, no forced account creation) is the single highest-impact feature. Online booking cart abandonment rates are notoriously high across all industries, so reducing friction at every step matters. |
| **Key details** | Support credit/debit cards at minimum. Show real-time slot availability to create urgency. Allow full booking completion without account creation. |

### 1.2 Room/Experience Pages

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Individual page (or detailed card) for each escape room, displaying: theme description, high-quality photos, difficulty level indicator, player count (min/max), duration, price, and a prominent "Book Now" CTA. |
| **Why** | Rooms are the product. Customers need to evaluate theme, difficulty, and group fit before committing. Escape rooms are experiential — compelling imagery and clear metadata (difficulty, duration, capacity) reduce pre-purchase anxiety and answer the questions visitors arrive with. |
| **Key details** | Display all rooms on the homepage or a single "Our Rooms" page so visitors never have to hunt. Use icons or visual indicators for difficulty (e.g., lock icons, star ratings) and player count. |

### 1.3 Pricing Display

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Clear, upfront pricing — either per person or per group — visible on room pages and/or a dedicated pricing section. |
| **Why** | Hidden pricing is the #1 friction point for experience-based businesses. Escape room customers comparison-shop across 2–3 local competitors. If they cannot find your price, they book elsewhere. Transparent pricing builds trust and reduces bounce rate. |
| **Key details** | If pricing varies by group size or time (peak/off-peak), show the range or a simple pricing table. |

### 1.4 Mobile-Responsive Design

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Fully responsive layout that works flawlessly on phones, tablets, and desktops. Mobile-first approach. |
| **Why** | Over 60% of web traffic now comes from mobile devices. Escape room bookings often happen spontaneously ("what should we do tonight?") from phones. A non-mobile-friendly site loses the majority of its potential customers. Google also penalizes non-responsive sites in search rankings. |

### 1.5 Location & Contact Information

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Physical address, embedded map (Google Maps), phone number, email, and hours of operation — visible on every page (typically in the footer) and on a dedicated Contact page. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms are local businesses. Customers need to know exactly where you are and how to reach you. NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency is also critical for local SEO ranking. An embedded map reduces "how do I get there?" friction and supports walk-in discovery. |

### 1.6 "How It Works" / First-Timer Section

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | A clear explanation of the escape room experience: what to expect, how games work, rules, what to bring, age requirements, arrival time guidance. |
| **Why** | A significant portion of visitors are first-time escape room players. Without this section, newcomers feel uncertain and are less likely to book. Reducing anxiety directly increases conversion. Best placed near the bottom of the homepage or as a standalone page. |

### 1.7 Basic SEO Foundation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Proper meta titles/descriptions, heading hierarchy, image alt text, fast page load speed, SSL certificate, XML sitemap, and mobile-friendliness. |
| **Why** | "Escape room near me" and "escape room in [city]" are high-intent local searches. If your site is not optimized for search engines, you are invisible to the customers most likely to book. Escape rooms rely heavily on organic local search traffic. |

### 1.8 Immersive Visual Design / Dark Theme Aesthetic

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Atmospheric, on-brand visual design that evokes mystery, adventure, or intrigue. Dark color palettes, cinematic photography, and intentional typography. |
| **Why** | The website is the first impression of the experience. Escape room customers are buying an atmosphere — if the website feels generic or cheap, they assume the rooms will too. Top-performing escape room websites consistently use dark, immersive themes with high-quality imagery. Design quality = perceived room quality. |

### 1.9 Analytics & Tracking

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Google Analytics (or equivalent) installed and configured with event tracking for key actions: booking starts, booking completions, gift card purchases, contact form submissions, and newsletter signups. |
| **Why** | You can't optimize what you don't measure. Understanding where visitors come from, which pages they visit, and where they drop off in the booking funnel is essential for improving conversion over time. Analytics data also informs marketing spend decisions. |

### 1.10 Legal Pages

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Cookie Policy. |
| **Why** | Legal requirements for any business collecting personal data and processing payments. Also required for Google Analytics, advertising pixels, and various third-party integrations. |

## 2. Strongly Recommended (Significant Impact on Bookings/Revenue)

These features have a proven, measurable impact on bookings, revenue, and customer satisfaction. Implement as soon as possible after launch.

### 2.1 Gift Cards / Vouchers (Online Purchase & Redemption)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Digital gift cards purchasable online, deliverable via email, and redeemable during the booking flow. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms are one of the top experiential gift purchases. Gift card revenue is collected upfront with zero fulfillment cost. Seasonal spikes (holidays, Valentine's Day, birthdays) drive major gift card volume. Not offering online gift cards leaves significant revenue on the table. |

### 2.2 Group & Private Booking Options

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Ability to book a room as a private session (no strangers mixed in) vs. a public/shared session. Clear indication of which option is selected, with appropriate pricing. |
| **Why** | "Will we be with strangers?" is the most common pre-booking concern. Many customers will only book if they can guarantee a private experience. Offering both public (fills rooms, maximizes revenue) and private (premium price, better experience) options serves all customer segments. |

### 2.3 Corporate & Team Building Page

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Dedicated landing page for corporate events and team building, with tailored messaging, group pricing info, inquiry form, and multi-room booking capabilities. |
| **Why** | Corporate events are the highest-value segment for escape rooms — larger groups, premium pricing, weekday bookings (filling off-peak slots), and repeat business. A dedicated page with professional messaging signals that you cater to this segment and captures leads that a generic booking flow would miss. |

### 2.4 Social Proof: Reviews & Testimonials

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Display of Google, TripAdvisor, and/or Yelp reviews on the website. Star ratings, review count, and selected testimonials. Award badges if applicable (e.g., TripAdvisor Travelers' Choice, "Best of [City]"). |
| **Why** | Escape rooms are experience-based purchases with no tangible preview — customers rely heavily on social proof before booking. Displaying your rating (e.g., "4.9 stars from 1,200+ reviews") builds immediate trust. Review scores are the single biggest trust signal for escape room shoppers. Rich review data in search results can meaningfully improve click-through rates. |

### 2.5 Google Business Profile Integration & Reviews

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Fully optimized Google Business Profile with accurate hours, photos, categories, booking link, and active review management. Website should link to and be consistent with GBP data. Display Google review rating and count on the website. |
| **Why** | GBP is the #1 local discovery channel for escape rooms. When someone searches "escape room near me," GBP results dominate the page before any website appears. An optimized GBP with a direct booking link can drive more traffic than the website homepage itself. Actively managing and displaying reviews builds trust both in search results and on the site. |

### 2.6 Digital Waiver System

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Online liability waiver that customers can sign digitally before arrival (via a link in their confirmation email) or on-site via tablet/kiosk. Waivers auto-linked to bookings and customer profiles. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms involve physical activity and enclosed spaces — liability waivers are standard practice. Pre-arrival digital waivers reduce check-in time from 10+ minutes to under 1 minute, improving the customer experience and reducing staff workload. Paper waivers are a logistics nightmare. |

### 2.7 Automated Email Confirmations & Reminders

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Booking confirmation email, pre-visit reminder (24h and/or 1h before), and post-visit follow-up email. |
| **Why** | No-shows are a significant revenue loss for escape rooms (a missed group = an empty room that cannot be resold). Automated reminders reduce no-shows dramatically. Pre-visit emails can include waiver links, parking instructions, and arrival guidelines. Post-visit emails drive reviews and repeat bookings. |

### 2.8 FAQ Page

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Frequently asked questions covering: cancellation/refund policy, parking, accessibility, lateness policy, group size flexibility, private vs. public bookings, age restrictions, and physical requirements. Can be combined with the "How It Works" section or standalone. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms generate many pre-booking questions unique to the format (Can we bring kids? What if we're late? Will we be locked in with strangers?). A thorough FAQ reduces support burden and removes last-minute booking hesitation. Also excellent for SEO when structured with FAQ schema markup. |

### 2.9 Newsletter / Email Capture

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Email signup form (inline, footer, or pop-up) offering an incentive (discount code, early access to new rooms). Integration with email marketing platform for campaigns and automations. |
| **Why** | Email is the highest-ROI marketing channel for escape rooms. Subscribers can be notified of new room launches, seasonal promotions, and events. A growing email list is a business asset that reduces dependence on paid advertising and gives you a direct line to past and potential customers. |

### 2.10 Accessibility Information

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Clear information about physical accessibility of the venue and individual rooms (wheelchair access, mobility requirements, claustrophobia considerations, hearing/vision accommodations). |
| **Why** | Escape rooms have unique accessibility considerations — narrow spaces, locked doors, physical puzzles. Proactively addressing these builds trust, avoids negative on-site experiences, and is increasingly expected by consumers. Also relevant for ADA compliance (WCAG 2.2 Level AA is the current standard). |

### 2.11 Structured Data / Schema Markup

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | JSON-LD structured data implementing LocalBusiness schema (with subtypes for entertainment), FAQ schema, Review schema, and Event schema. |
| **Why** | Schema markup enables rich snippets in Google search results — star ratings, price ranges, FAQ dropdowns, and business hours appear directly in the search listing, which can meaningfully improve click-through rates. Most competing escape rooms do not implement schema markup, making it a competitive advantage. |

### 2.12 404 / Error Page

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | A branded, on-theme 404 page with helpful navigation (links to homepage, rooms, booking) instead of a generic browser error. |
| **Why** | Broken links and mistyped URLs happen. A generic 404 is a dead end that loses the visitor. A branded error page with clear navigation keeps them on the site and can even be a fun on-brand moment ("You didn't escape this page..."). |

## 3. Nice-to-Have (Competitive Differentiators)

These features separate good escape room websites from great ones. They improve customer experience, increase repeat business, and create word-of-mouth.

### 3.1 Birthday Party / Special Events Page

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Dedicated page for birthday parties and celebrations, with party package details, add-ons (decorations, food, cake), group minimums, and an inquiry/booking form. |
| **Why** | Birthday parties are a top driver of group bookings and introduce new players who become repeat customers. Parents and event planners search specifically for "escape room birthday party [city]." A dedicated page captures this high-intent traffic. Can be handled through the corporate/group inquiry form initially. |

### 3.2 Photo Gallery

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Standalone gallery page with high-quality photos of rooms (without spoilers), lobby/waiting area, team celebration photos, and themed detail shots. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms sell an atmosphere. A dedicated gallery goes beyond the photos on individual room pages, showcasing the overall vibe of the venue and happy customer moments. Customer photos from post-game celebrations serve as powerful social proof. |

### 3.3 Post-Game Photo Sharing System

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Branded team photos taken after each game, uploaded to a gallery or sent via text/email, with social sharing buttons and branded overlays. |
| **Why** | The post-game celebration photo is the most shared moment of the escape room experience. When teams share branded photos on social media, they generate free organic marketing. Auto-branded overlays ensure your business name is visible in every share. This feature turns every customer into a marketing channel. |

### 3.4 Leaderboard / Hall of Fame

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Public leaderboard displaying top escape times by room, filterable by group size, time period (last 30 days / all-time), and sortable by time, points, or clues used. |
| **Why** | Leaderboards add a competitive element that drives repeat bookings ("we need to beat our time") and social sharing ("we got #3 on the leaderboard!"). They also create aspirational content for first-time visitors and extend the engagement beyond the physical visit. |

### 3.5 Blog / Content Hub

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Regularly updated blog with posts covering: escape room tips for beginners, team building advice, behind-the-scenes content, local event roundups, puzzle-solving strategies, and themed content aligned with room launches. |
| **Why** | Blog content targets long-tail SEO keywords that the main site pages cannot capture (e.g., "escape room tips for beginners," "best team building activities in [city]," "what to expect at an escape room"). Each blog post is a new entry point from organic search. Content marketing also positions the brand as an authority in the space. |

### 3.6 Live Chat / Chatbot

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Real-time chat widget for visitor questions, with AI chatbot handling common queries (hours, pricing, availability) and live agent handoff for complex questions. |
| **Why** | Many customers have quick pre-booking questions that prevent them from completing a booking. A chat widget answers these questions in real time without requiring a phone call or email. AI chatbots can operate 24/7 and in multiple languages, serving international tourists and late-night planners. |

### 3.7 Upsells & Add-Ons During Checkout

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Add-on options presented during the booking flow: photo packages, merchandise, hint packages, food/beverage add-ons, or VIP upgrades. |
| **Why** | The checkout moment is when purchase intent is highest. Presenting relevant add-ons increases average transaction value with minimal friction. Escape rooms have natural upsell opportunities (branded merch, photo packages, extra clues) that complement the core experience. |

### 3.8 Loyalty / Rewards Program

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Points-based or quest-based system where customers earn rewards for bookings, referrals, and engagement. Tiers (Bronze, Silver, Gold) unlock perks like discounts, priority booking, or free games. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms have a natural repeat-play loop — customers want to try every room. A loyalty program formalizes this by rewarding return visits. Quest-based loyalty (collect puzzle pieces to unlock a reward) is uniquely aligned with the escape room brand. |

### 3.9 Referral Program

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Shareable referral codes/links that give the referrer and their friend a discount or reward. Trackable through unique codes. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms are inherently social — players bring groups of friends and family. Referral programs formalize the word-of-mouth that already drives the business, and make it trackable. Offering a referral discount (e.g., "Refer 5 friends, get a free game") turns satisfied customers into an active acquisition channel. |

### 3.10 Abandoned Cart / Booking Recovery

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Automated email or notification triggered when a customer starts the booking process but does not complete payment. |
| **Why** | Cart abandonment rates in online booking are high across all industries. Recovery emails can recapture a meaningful percentage of lost bookings. Typically handled by the booking platform (Resova supports this) rather than built into the website itself. More impactful at higher booking volume. |

### 3.11 Social Media Integration & UGC Display

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Social media links, Instagram feed embed, branded hashtag display, and curated user-generated content (customer photos and videos) on the website. |
| **Why** | Escape room experiences are highly shareable. Integrating social proof from real customers (tagged photos, stories, reels) onto the website provides authentic, continuously refreshing content. Branded hashtags (e.g., #EscapeLockAndLogic) aggregate UGC and build community. |

### 3.12 Video Content (Trailer / Promo)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Short cinematic trailer or promo video for each room (or the venue overall), embedded on the homepage and room pages. |
| **Why** | Video conveys atmosphere more effectively than photos or text. A 30–60 second room trailer creates excitement and emotional buy-in that static images cannot match. Video also increases time-on-site (an SEO signal) and performs well on social media for paid and organic distribution. |

## 4. Advanced (For Growth/Scaling)

These features support escape room businesses as they grow, scale to multiple locations, or pursue franchise operations.

### 4.1 Dynamic Pricing Engine

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Automated price adjustments based on demand signals: day of week, time of day, lead time before session, historical occupancy, and group size. |
| **Why** | Escape rooms have fixed capacity (X rooms, Y slots per day). Dynamic pricing maximizes revenue per slot — charging more during peak times (Friday/Saturday evenings) and less during off-peak (Tuesday afternoons) to fill otherwise empty rooms. Properly implemented dynamic pricing can meaningfully increase revenue without increasing capacity. |

### 4.2 Multi-Location Management

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Centralized dashboard managing multiple venue locations from a single admin interface, with location-specific pages, booking flows, staff permissions, and reporting. |
| **Why** | As an escape room business expands to multiple venues, managing separate websites and booking systems becomes unsustainable. A unified platform with per-location controls reduces operational complexity and maintains brand consistency. Staff can be assigned location-specific permissions. |

### 4.3 Advanced Analytics & Reporting Dashboard

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Custom dashboards tracking: booking conversion rate, revenue by room/day/channel, occupancy rates, average group size, no-show rates, customer lifetime value, marketing attribution, and seasonal trends. Exportable reports. |
| **Why** | Data-driven decisions are critical for scaling. Knowing which rooms are most popular, which marketing channels drive the most bookings, and which time slots consistently underperform allows owners to optimize pricing, marketing spend, and room development. |

### 4.4 CRM / Customer Database

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Centralized customer profiles linking booking history, waiver records, photo gallery, email engagement, loyalty status, and lifetime value. Segmentation capabilities for targeted marketing. |
| **Why** | Understanding your customers enables personalization — targeting first-time visitors differently from loyal regulars, identifying at-risk customers who haven't returned, and creating high-value segments (corporate bookers, birthday planners). A CRM transforms one-time transactions into ongoing relationships. |

### 4.5 Staff / Game Master Scheduling Integration

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Staff scheduling tied to booking calendar — automatically ensuring adequate game master coverage for booked sessions, with shift management, availability tracking, and labor cost reporting. |
| **Why** | Game masters are the critical human resource in escape rooms. Understaffing ruins the experience; overstaffing wastes payroll. Integrated scheduling ensures the right number of game masters are scheduled based on actual bookings, not guesswork. |

### 4.6 API / Third-Party Integrations

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | API access and integrations with: OTAs (TripAdvisor Experiences, Viator, GetYourGuide), POS systems, accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero), CRM platforms, and marketing tools (Mailchimp, Klaviyo, Google Ads). |
| **Why** | As the business scales, it needs to connect its booking system to the broader tech stack. OTA integrations expand distribution. POS integration handles walk-ins and merchandise. Accounting integration automates financial reconciliation. These integrations reduce manual work and prevent data silos. |

### 4.7 Franchise / White-Label Capabilities

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Templated site generation for franchise locations, centralized brand control with local customization, franchise-level reporting, and royalty/commission tracking. |
| **Why** | The escape room franchise market is growing, with single-unit, multi-unit, and area development models. A white-label platform that maintains brand consistency while allowing franchisees to manage local content and bookings is essential for franchise scalability. |

### 4.8 A/B Testing & Conversion Optimization Tools

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Built-in or integrated tools for testing variations of CTAs, pricing displays, page layouts, and booking flows to identify highest-converting versions. |
| **Why** | Small improvements in booking conversion rate have outsized revenue impact. Testing different CTA button colors, price display formats, or booking flow steps can yield meaningful conversion improvements. At scale, this translates to significant additional bookings per year without increased marketing spend. |

### 4.9 Automated Review Solicitation & Reputation Management

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Post-visit automated emails/texts prompting customers to leave a review on Google, TripAdvisor, or Yelp. Smart routing (send happy customers to public review sites, unhappy customers to private feedback). Centralized dashboard monitoring reviews across all platforms. |
| **Why** | Review volume and recency are critical ranking factors for local search. Automating review requests ensures a steady stream of fresh reviews. Smart routing protects the public rating while still capturing valuable negative feedback privately. Most escape rooms that dominate local search have aggressive review solicitation systems. |

### 4.10 Inventory & Merchandise E-Commerce

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Online store for branded merchandise (t-shirts, puzzles, mugs), physical gift cards, and themed products. Integrated with the main site. |
| **Why** | Merchandise extends the brand beyond the physical experience and creates an additional revenue stream. Customers who had a great experience want to take something home. An online store also serves as a marketing touchpoint — every t-shirt is a walking advertisement. |

### 4.11 Multi-Language Support

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What** | Website and booking flow available in multiple languages. |
| **Why** | Only relevant for escape rooms in tourist-heavy markets serving significant international audiences. Language barriers at the booking stage lose customers. Not applicable for most single-location businesses in non-metro areas. |
