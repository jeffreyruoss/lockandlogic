> Client-facing document | February 2026

This is the full SEO strategy for Lock & Logic's website and online presence. It covers everything from the technical foundation that should be built into the site, to the ongoing content and marketing activities that drive bookings through organic search.

## Why SEO Matters for Lock & Logic

When someone in Pottstown searches "escape room near me" or "things to do this weekend," you want Lock & Logic to be the first result they see. That's what SEO does — it makes your business visible to people who are already looking for what you offer.

Here's the good news:

- **There is no escape room in Pottstown right now.** Captured in Coventry (Coventry Mall) permanently closed. You have zero direct local competition.
- **The closest competitors are 7+ miles away** (Trappe and Limerick), and the major cluster is 22+ miles away in King of Prussia.
- **Pottstown has very few indoor entertainment options** — only about 7 total according to activity directories. You'll stand out.
- **There is proven demand.** Captured in Coventry had strong reviews before closing, and people are still searching for escape rooms in the Pottstown area.

This strategy is designed to make Lock & Logic the dominant search result for escape rooms in western Montgomery County and the surrounding area.

## Part 1: Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is arguably more important than your website for local discovery. When someone searches "escape room near me," the Google Map results (the "map pack") appear before any website. **46% of all Google searches have local intent**, and 75% of local searchers visit a business within 24 hours.

### Recommended Setup

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Primary category** | "Escape Room Center" (Google's dedicated category for escape rooms — this is the single strongest ranking signal) |
| **Secondary categories** | Entertainment Company, Team Building Service, Tourist Attraction, Party Entertainment Service |
| **Service area** | Pottstown, West Pottsgrove, Lower Pottsgrove, Upper Pottsgrove, North Coventry, East Coventry, Limerick, Royersford, Spring City, Phoenixville, Collegeville, Trappe, Gilbertsville, Boyertown |
| **Services listed** | Each room listed individually with description and pricing |
| **Booking link** | Direct link to your booking page |
| **Business description** | Keyword-rich description mentioning "escape room in Pottstown, PA" naturally |
| **Photos** | Room photos, lobby, exterior, team celebration shots |
| **Q&A** | Pre-seed common questions (How long is a game? What ages? Can we book privately?) |

### Pre-Opening Advantage

Google allows you to create your profile **up to 90 days before opening**. That means it can be set up in February/March and start appearing in search results before the May opening. During this period, construction updates, room teasers, and anticipation-building posts can go out.

### Ongoing GBP Activity

- Post updates weekly (promotions, events, behind-the-scenes photos)
- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- Upload new photos regularly
- Keep hours updated, especially for holidays

## Part 3: On-Page SEO (Built Into the Website)

These are the technical SEO elements that should be built into the Astro site. Once in place, they work automatically.

### Recommended Elements

| Element | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| **Meta titles & descriptions** | Every page gets a unique, keyword-rich title and description that appears in Google search results. |
| **Heading hierarchy** | Proper H1 → H2 → H3 structure on every page so Google understands your content. |
| **Image alt text** | Descriptive text on every image so Google can "see" your photos. Also helps accessibility. |
| **XML sitemap** | A file that tells Google about every page on your site so nothing gets missed. |
| **Robots.txt** | Tells search engines which pages to index and which to skip. |
| **SSL certificate** | The padlock icon in the browser. Required for SEO and customer trust. |
| **Page speed optimization** | Astro ships zero JavaScript by default — your site will be faster than almost every competitor (most use WordPress). |
| **Mobile-first design** | Google uses the mobile version of your site for ranking. A mobile-first approach means the site is optimized from the start. |
| **Internal linking** | Every page links to related pages (rooms link to booking, blog posts link to rooms) so Google can crawl efficiently and customers can navigate easily. |
| **URL structure** | Clean, readable URLs: `/rooms/the-vault` instead of `/page?id=123`. |

### Structured Data (Schema Markup)

This is behind-the-scenes code that tells Google exactly what your business is. It enables "rich snippets" — those enhanced search results that show star ratings, price ranges, hours, and FAQ answers directly in Google.

**Recommended schema types:**

| Schema Type | What It Shows in Google |
|-------------|------------------------|
| **EntertainmentBusiness** (LocalBusiness) | Your business name, address, phone, hours, and price range in search results. |
| **FAQPage** | Your FAQ answers appear as expandable dropdowns directly in search results. |
| **Event** | Special events and seasonal experiences show up in Google's event listings. |
| **AggregateRating** | Your star rating and review count appear next to your listing. |
| **BreadcrumbList** | Clean navigation path shown in search results (Home > Rooms > The Vault). |

Most of your competitors don't implement structured data. Only Expedition Escape (King of Prussia) does it well. This is a real competitive advantage.

### NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone Number. Search engines cross-reference this information across the entire web to verify your business is legitimate. Inconsistent NAP data (even small differences like "Street" vs "St.") can hurt your rankings.

**The canonical format should be defined once and used everywhere:**

```
Lock & Logic
[Full Street Address], Pottstown, PA [ZIP]
(XXX) XXX-XXXX
```

This exact format should be used on the website footer (every page), Google Business Profile, every directory listing, and all social media profiles. Quarterly audits catch inconsistencies before they hurt rankings.

## Part 5: Review Strategy

Reviews are one of the most heavily weighted factors in local search rankings. **Review recency** (how recently you've received reviews) is called the most underrated ranking factor by leading SEO researchers. For a brand-new business with zero reviews, building a review base quickly is essential.

### Pre-Opening: Seed Initial Reviews

- **Soft launch / beta sessions**: Invite friends, family, local influencers, and community leaders for free pre-opening playthroughs. Ask them to leave honest reviews on Google.
- **Local business partners**: Invite partner business owners for preview sessions.
- **Media previews**: Invite local bloggers and press for early access.

### Post-Opening: Systematic Review Collection

Every group that plays should be asked for a review. Make it part of your standard process:

1. **Post-game photo moment**: Group photos with a branded backdrop. Teams holding a "We Escaped!" sign are psychologically primed to leave positive reviews.
2. **QR code at the exit**: A visible QR code linking directly to your Google review page. Guests scan it while the excitement is fresh.
3. **Automated follow-up email**: Resova can send a post-game email within 1-2 hours that includes their group photo, escape time, and a direct "Leave a Review" link.
4. **Game master ask**: Train game masters to personally thank each group and mention that reviews help the business. A genuine, human ask is more effective than any automation.

### Review Management

- **Respond to every review** — positive and negative — within 24 hours. This is both a ranking signal and a trust signal.
- **Goal**: 50+ five-star reviews within the first 3 months of opening.
- **Rotate platforms**: Focus on Google first (most impactful for SEO), then TripAdvisor and Yelp.

### What Not to Do

- Never buy fake reviews (Google will detect and penalize this)
- Never offer discounts explicitly in exchange for reviews (violates Google's terms)
- Never "review-gate" (only sending happy customers to review sites)
